My Thinking Style

July 13, 2015

I took this quiz and found out that my Gregorc Thinking Style is "Abstract sequential".

What does this mean? Well, apparently I like my point to be heard; analyzing the situation before trying to find a solution; and using logic to solve problems. I learn best when I have access to mentors, experts, and when I'm placed in stimulating environments, and when I'm able to work alone.

What's hard for me is where it gets interesting:Being forced to work with people of differing views; too litte time to deal with the subject thoroughly; repeating the same tasks over and over; lots of specific rules/regulations; "sentimental thinking", i.e. expressing my emotions; Being diplomatic when convincing others; not monopolizing the conversation

I have to say...this describes me pretty darn well! I think these tendencies have manifested with my DBC learning in a few ways. I'll talk about the positives first: I'm really good at pseudocoding! And I enjoy it. My tendencies to analyzing a situation before acting is exactly what pseudocoding is about, and several students have mentioned they learned a lot about pseudocoding after pairing with me.

One place where I can struggle is when I feel like my opinion is not being heard, i.e. when someone else is monopolizing the conversation. This makes me feel very uncomfortable and even downright angry. I find myself getting judgmental as well. So, the ability to listen to others is something I could really work on.

Another case where I struggle is with doing actions which are routine, which I have to do over and over again. This applies to following best practices, such as with doing my "reflection" exercises. I frequently procrastinate on those. I also abhor the git push/pull process. I find myself procrastinating on actually pushing my changes and doing the pull requests. So another place for me to improve is to be more disciplined about the small actions which I must do routinely. It is important for me to stay on top of these actions.
